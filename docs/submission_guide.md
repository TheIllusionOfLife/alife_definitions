## 7. Abstract

*(Copy from paper, plain text, ~130 words)*

Multiple operational definitions of life exist, yet they are rarely applied to the same system under controlled conditions. We present an empirical benchmark comparing four definitions -- textbook 7-criteria (D1), Darwinian/NASA (D2), autonomy/organizational closure (D3), and information maintenance (D4) -- by scoring the same organism populations across five environment regimes in a shared digital ecology. Three co-existing family types with different ablated capabilities serve as natural controls. We report the disagreement structure among definitions, identify regime-dependent divergences, and evaluate predictive validity against population robustness within our substrate and prediction targets. Evaluation mode substantially affects predictive ranking: under strict (leakage-aware) evaluation, D4's AUC drops from 0.78 to 0.54 while D1 remains at 0.85, revealing that D4's causal component currently captures population-level rather than organism-level information maintenance. Our benchmark suite and adapter code will be released as an open artifact for the ALife community.
